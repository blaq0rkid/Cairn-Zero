## INTRODUCTION: WHAT IS CAIRN ZERO? {#introduction}

Cairn Zero solves a critical problem faced by business owners, lawyers, and professionals: **What happens to your business if you're suddenly unavailable?**

### The Problem: The Founder Immortality Trap

Most businesses operate as if the founder will always be available. Critical passwords, client relationships, and operational knowledge exist only in the founder's head or personal devices. When the founder becomes unavailable—due to illness, accident, or death—the business grinds to a halt.

### The Solution: Your Digital Cairn

A **Cairn** is a stack of stones used as a trail marker. Similarly, Cairn Zero creates a secure, encrypted marker that guides your designated successors to your business's "keys to the kingdom" when you can no longer provide them yourself.

### How It Works (Simple Version)

1. You encrypt critical information on your device
2. You designate trusted successors
3. You check in regularly (we remind you)
4. If you don't check in, your successors automatically receive access
5. No one—including Cairn Zero—can access your data except your successors

## FOR FOUNDERS: GETTING STARTED {#for-founders}

### Step 1: Create Your Account

**What You Need:**
- A valid email address
- A device with biometric authentication (fingerprint, Face ID, or Windows Hello)
- 15 minutes

**The Process:**

1. **Enter Your Email**
   - Visit cairnzero.com/signup
   - Enter your business email address
   - Check your inbox for a 6-digit verification code

2. **Verify Your Email**
   - Enter the code within 10 minutes
   - This confirms you control the email address

3. **Create Your Passkey**
   - Your device will prompt you to use your fingerprint or face
   - This creates a cryptographic key that stays on your device
   - **Important:** Cairn Zero never sees or stores this key

4. **Confirm Your Understanding**
   - Review the Zero-Knowledge Sovereignty statement
   - Acknowledge that you maintain complete control
   - Accept that Cairn Zero cannot recover lost credentials

### Step 2: Designate Your Successors

**Who Should Be a Successor?**
- Business partners
- Family members with business knowledge
- Trusted employees
- Professional advisors (lawyers, accountants)

**How Many Successors?**
- Minimum: 1
- Recommended: 2-3
- Maximum: 5

**Adding a Successor:**

1. Click "Add Successor" in your dashboard
2. Enter their full name and email address
3. Choose their priority order (1st, 2nd, 3rd)
4. Add optional instructions specific to them
5. Click "Send Invitation"

**What Happens Next?**
- Your successor receives an email invitation
- They create their own secure credentials
- They complete a "rehearsal" to verify the system works
- You're notified when they're ready

### Step 3: Create Your Cairn (Protected Information)

**What to Include:**
- Critical passwords (email, banking, CRM)
- Client contact information
- Vendor credentials
- Important file locations
- Emergency procedures
- Banking information
- Legal document locations

**How to Create a Cairn:**

1. Navigate to "Create Cairn" in your dashboard
2. Type or paste your information in the text box
3. Review what you've entered
4. Click "Encrypt & Save"

**What Happens Behind the Scenes:**
- Your browser encrypts the data before sending anything
- The encrypted data is stored in our secure database
- Your successors receive a special "key" to unlock it
- Even we cannot read your encrypted data

**Best Practices:**
- Use clear, simple language
- Include context ("This password is for...")
- Update information when it changes
- Test with a rehearsal annually

### Step 4: Set Your Check-In Frequency

**Why Check-Ins?**

Regular check-ins prove you're still active and in control. If you miss your check-in window, the succession process begins.

**Frequency Options:**
- Weekly (for high-risk situations)
- Monthly (default)
- Quarterly (for stable situations)
- Custom (30-365 days)

**How Check-Ins Work:**

1. We send you an email reminder before your deadline
2. You click a link in the email or log into your dashboard
3. You confirm you're still in control
4. The countdown resets

**What If You Miss a Check-In?**
- First reminder: 7 days before deadline
- Second reminder: 24 hours before deadline
- Final reminder: 1 hour before deadline
- If deadline passes: Succession process begins

### Step 5: Complete the Succession Rehearsal

**What is a Rehearsal?**

Before your Cairn becomes active, you must verify that your successors can actually access information when needed. This is a **mandatory** test using non-critical data.

**The Rehearsal Process:**

1. You create a test message (not real passwords)
2. The system sends it to your designated successor
3. Your successor opens and decrypts the message
4. You receive confirmation that it worked
5. Your Cairn becomes fully active

**Why This Matters:**

You need certainty that your succession plan works. The rehearsal provides proof that:
- Your successor can receive notifications
- They know how to access the system
- The encryption/decryption works correctly
- You'll be notified if there's a problem

**Timeline:**
- You cannot skip this step
- Most rehearsals complete within 24 hours
- If your successor doesn't respond, you'll be notified
- You can retry with a different successor if needed

## FOR SUCCESSORS: YOUR ROLE {#for-successors}

### What Does It Mean to Be a Successor?

You've been designated by a business owner to receive critical information if they become unavailable. This is a position of trust and responsibility.

### Your Responsibilities

**Before a Succession Event:**
- Respond to the invitation promptly
- Complete the succession rehearsal
- Keep your credentials secure
- Update your contact information if it changes

**During a Succession Event:**
- Check your email regularly
- Respond quickly when notified
- Handle information with confidentiality
- Follow any specific instructions provided

**After Receiving Access:**
- Use information only for intended business purposes
- Protect sensitive data
- Coordinate with other successors (if applicable)
- Document actions taken for legal clarity

### How to Accept Your Role

**Step 1: Receive the Invitation**
- Check your email for an invitation from Cairn Zero
- Subject line: "You've been designated as a successor"
- Verify the sender is the person you know

**Step 2: Create Your Credentials**
- Click the link in the email
- Set up your own passkey (biometric authentication)
- This is separate from the founder's credentials
- No passwords to remember

**Step 3: Complete the Rehearsal**
- You'll receive a test message from the founder
- Open it using your new credentials
- Confirm successful decryption
- The founder will be notified

**Step 4: Wait for Activation**
- You're now an active successor
- You'll only receive access if the founder:
  - Misses their check-in deadline, OR
  - Manually grants access, OR
  - Becomes verified as incapacitated

### What Happens in a Succession Event?

**Notification:**
- You receive an email: "Succession event triggered"
- You'll see a secure link to access the Cairn
- Access is typically available within 15 minutes

**Accessing the Information:**
1. Click the secure link
2. Authenticate with your passkey
3. The system decrypts the information in your browser
4. You can view, copy, or save the information
5. The founder is notified (if possible)

**Important Notes:**
- Access is logged for security
- Information is decrypted only on your device
- Cairn Zero cannot see the decrypted data
- Use information ethically and legally

## SECURITY & PRIVACY {#security-privacy}

### Zero-Knowledge Architecture

**What This Means:**

Cairn Zero operates on a "zero-knowledge" basis. We provide the infrastructure but never have access to your actual data.

**How It Works:**

1. **Encryption Happens on Your Device**
   - Your browser encrypts data before sending
   - We never receive unencrypted information
   - Industry-standard AES-256 encryption

2. **No Master Keys**
   - We don't have a "back door"
   - We cannot decrypt your data
   - Even if legally compelled, we have nothing to provide

3. **Passkey Security**
   - Your passkey stays on your device
   - Uses biometric authentication (fingerprint, Face ID)
   - Cannot be stolen through phishing
   - Cannot be guessed or brute-forced

### What We Do Store

**We Store:**
- Your email address
- Encrypted data (meaningless without your key)
- Check-in timestamps
- Successor designations
- System activity logs

**We Never Store:**
- Your passkey private key
- Decrypted Cairn contents
- Passwords or sensitive credentials
- Biometric data

### Privacy Guarantees

- **No Data Mining:** We don't analyze or sell your data
- **No Advertising:** We're a paid service, not ad-supported
- **No Third-Party Sharing:** Except required infrastructure (AWS, email delivery)
- **Right to Deletion:** You can delete your account and all data anytime

### Security Best Practices

**For Founders:**
1. Use a device with biometric authentication
2. Keep your device's operating system updated
3. Don't share your device's unlock credentials
4. Review successor list regularly
5. Update information when it changes
6. Complete annual rehearsals

**For Successors:**
1. Keep your credentials private
2. Don't access information unless authorized
3. Use secure connections (never public WiFi)
4. Log out when finished
5. Report suspicious activity immediately

## CHECK-IN SYSTEM {#check-in-system}

### How the Check-In System Works

The check-in system is your "heartbeat" that proves you're active and in control.

**The Cycle:**

```
[Check-In] → [Countdown Starts] → [Reminder Emails] → [Deadline] → [Succession Trigger]
```

### Check-In Methods

**Method 1: Email Link (Easiest)**
1. Receive reminder email
2. Click "I'm Still Here" button
3. Done—countdown resets

**Method 2: Dashboard Login**
1. Log into cairnzero.com
2. Click "Check In" button
3. Countdown resets

**Method 3: Mobile App (Coming Soon)**
- Quick check-in from your phone
- Biometric authentication
- Push notification reminders

### Customizing Your Frequency

**Access Settings:**
1. Log into your dashboard
2. Navigate to Settings → Check-In Frequency
3. Select your preferred interval
4. Changes take effect immediately

**Choosing the Right Frequency:**

- **Weekly:** High-risk activities, solo operations
- **Monthly:** Standard for most businesses
- **Quarterly:** Established businesses with partners
- **Custom:** Seasonal businesses, specific needs

### Missed Check-In Protocol

**What Happens If You Miss Your Deadline:**

**Immediately:**
- System marks check-in as missed
- Grace period begins (24 hours)
- Emergency contact notified (if configured)

**After 24 Hours:**
- Succession verification process starts
- We may attempt phone contact (if provided)
- Successors notified to stand by

**After 48 Hours:**
- If no response, succession triggers
- Successors granted access
- All activity logged

**False Alarm?**
- Log in immediately
- Click "I'm Here—False Alarm"
- Access to successors is revoked
- System resets to normal

### Pausing Check-Ins

**Planned Absence (Vacation, Surgery):**

1. Navigate to Settings → Pause Check-Ins
2. Select start and end dates
3. Add optional note
4. Confirm pause

**Important:**
- Maximum pause: 90 days
- You'll receive reminder before auto-resume
- Can cancel pause anytime
- Successors are notified of pause

## SUCCESSION REHEARSAL {#succession-rehearsal}

### Why Rehearsals Are Mandatory

You need absolute certainty that your succession plan works. Testing with real data is too risky. Rehearsals provide proof of functionality without exposing sensitive information.

### The Rehearsal Process (Detailed)

**Phase 1: Setup (Founder)**

1. Log into your dashboard
2. Click "Start Succession Rehearsal"
3. Select which successor to test
4. Write a test message (examples below)
5. Click "Send Test Marker"

**Phase 2: Notification (System)**

- Email sent to successor
- Subject: "Succession Rehearsal—Test Marker Ready"
- Contains secure link
- Valid for 72 hours

**Phase 3: Unwrap (Successor)**

1. Successor clicks link in email
2. Authenticates with their passkey
3. Decrypts test message
4. Confirms successful decryption
5. System logs completion

**Phase 4: Confirmation (Both Parties)**

- Founder receives email: "Rehearsal Complete ✓"
- Successor receives confirmation
- System marks rehearsal as passed
- Onboarding gate unlocked

### Test Message Examples

**Example 1: Simple**
```
This is a test succession message. If you can read this, 
the system is working correctly. No action needed.
```

**Example 2: With Instructions**
```
REHEARSAL TEST

If you're reading this, the succession system works!

In a real succession event, this would contain:
- Critical passwords
- Client contacts
- Emergency procedures

For now, just confirm you can read this message.
```

**Example 3: Verifiable**
```
Rehearsal Code: ALPHA-2026-CHARLIE

Please reply to this email with the rehearsal code 
to confirm successful decryption.
```

### Troubleshooting Rehearsals

**Problem: Successor didn't receive email**

- Check spam/junk folder
- Verify email address is correct
- Resend test marker
- Try different email address

**Problem: Successor can't authenticate**

- Ensure they completed setup
- Check device compatibility
- Try different browser
- Contact support

**Problem: Decryption fails**

- System error—not user error
- Contact support immediately
- Do NOT retry multiple times
- We'll investigate backend logs

**Problem: Test passed but founder not notified**

- Check your spam folder
- Verify your email address
- Check notification settings
- Contact support to verify logs

### Annual Rehearsal Recommendation

Even after initial setup, we recommend annual rehearsals:

**Benefits:**
- Confirms successors still have access
- Updates contact information
- Refreshes successor's memory
- Tests any system updates
- Provides peace of mind

**How to Schedule:**
1. Set calendar reminder for 1 year
2. Run rehearsal same time each year
3. Update information if needed
4. Document completion

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS {#faq}

### General Questions

**Q: What happens if I lose my device?**

A: Your passkey is stored in your device's secure enclave. If you lose your device:
- Set up a new device with the same biometric
- Your passkey can be recovered via iCloud Keychain (Apple) or Google Password Manager
- If completely lost, contact support for account recovery process
- This may require identity verification and successor coordination

**Q: Can I have multiple devices?**

A: Yes! Passkeys can sync across devices using:
- iCloud Keychain (Apple devices)
- Google Password Manager (Android)
- Windows Hello (Microsoft)
- Hardware security keys (YubiKey, etc.)

**Q: What if my business partner and I both use Cairn Zero?**

A: Perfect! You can:
- Designate each other as successors
- Create mutual backup arrangements
- Each maintain separate Cairns
- Coordinate check-in frequencies

**Q: How much does Cairn Zero cost?**

A: Pricing tiers (visit cairnzero.com/pricing):
- Solo: $29/month (1 founder, 3 successors)
- Professional: $79/month (1 founder, 10 successors)
- Enterprise: Custom pricing (multiple founders)

All plans include:
- Unlimited Cairns
- Unlimited check-ins
- Annual rehearsals
- Email support
- 99.9% uptime guarantee

**Q: Can I cancel anytime?**

A: Yes. No long-term contracts. However:
- Your data remains encrypted and accessible
- You must complete final check-in
- Successors are notified of cancellation
- Data deleted 30 days after cancellation (unless requested sooner)

### Security Questions

**Q: What if someone hacks Cairn Zero's servers?**

A: They would only find encrypted data. Without your passkey (which we don't have), the data is mathematically unreadable. This is the core of our zero-knowledge architecture.

**Q: What if the government demands access to my data?**

A: We can only provide what we have: encrypted data. We have no ability to decrypt it. We would comply with lawful requests but the data would be useless without your passkey.

**Q: Can Cairn Zero employees see my data?**

A: No. Our employees have the same access as hackers: encrypted data only. We've designed the system to make it technically impossible for us to access your information.

**Q: What happens if Cairn Zero goes out of business?**

A: We have a "Dead Man's Switch" for ourselves:
- 90-day advance notice to all users
- Open-source release of decryption tools
- Data export functionality
- Continuation fund to maintain servers during transition
- Partnership with archive.org for long-term data preservation

### Technical Questions

**Q: What encryption do you use?**

A: 
- AES-256-GCM for data encryption
- RSA-4096-OAEP for key wrapping
- All encryption happens client-side in your browser
- Industry-standard Web Crypto API

**Q: Do you support two-factor authentication?**

A: Passkeys ARE multi-factor authentication:
- Something you have (your device)
- Something you are (your biometric)
- More secure than traditional 2FA

**Q: What browsers are supported?**

A: WebAuthn is supported in:
- Chrome 67+ (all platforms)
- Safari 13+ (macOS, iOS)
- Firefox 60+ (all platforms)
- Edge 18+ (Windows, macOS)

**Q: Can I use this on my phone?**

A: Yes! Mobile browsers fully support passkeys:
- iOS Safari (iOS 16+)
- Chrome (Android 9+)
- Samsung Internet
- Full feature parity with desktop

### Succession Questions

**Q: Can I test the system without actually triggering succession?**

A: Yes—that's what the succession rehearsal is for. It uses test data, not real credentials.

**Q: What if my successor dies before me?**

A: 
- Remove them from your successor list
- Designate a replacement
- Run a new rehearsal
- Update any instructions that referenced them

**Q: Can successors access my data before I'm gone?**

A: No. Access is only granted when:
- You miss check-in deadline, OR
- You manually grant access, OR
- Death certificate is submitted (with verification)

**Q: What proof of death is required?**

A: For manual triggering (not automatic):
- Death certificate (certified copy)
- Identification verification of successor
- 48-hour verification period
- Notarized affidavit (for high-value accounts)

**Q: Can I revoke a successor's access?**

A: Yes, anytime before succession triggers:
- Log into your dashboard
- Navigate to Successors
- Click "Revoke Access"
- Successor is immediately notified
- Their credentials are invalidated
